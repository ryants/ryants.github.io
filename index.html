Ryan Suderman

Computational Biologist

Education and Technical Skills

Currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Los Alamos National Laboratory, I received my B.A. in Mathematics from Goshen College in Indiana and graduated with a Ph.D. in Computational Biology from the University of Kansas. I am an experienced user of numerous methods and software frameworks for modeling dynamical systems. I am also proficient with the Python and Scala programming languages, Unix-like utilities, and the bash shell.

Research Experience and Interests

As a member of the Center for Nonlinear Studies and the Theoretical Biology and Biophysics Group at Los Alamos National Lab, I build and analyze dynamical models of intracellular signaling networks using a variety of methodologies, including rule-based modeling languages (domain-specific languages for modeling biochemical reaction networks) such as Kappa and BioNetGen. My research interests lie primarily in understanding how cells use complex protein interaction networks to process information about their environment both on an abstract level and for specific disease-relevant biological systems

Tool Development for Scientific Research

Performing computational research often requires custom tools and libraries. I continue to develop and maintain a number of applications designed facilitate data analysis and model construction for dynamical systems modeling. These include an application to estimate information theoretic quantities from cellular signaling data (EstCC) and a program that translates between models written in distinct rule-based modeling languages
